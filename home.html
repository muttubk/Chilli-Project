MORE ABOUT CHILLI

Chilli, also known as chili or chili pepper, is a fruit of the plant species Capsicum. It is commonly used as a spice in many different cuisines around the world, and is known for its pungent and spicy flavor. Chilli peppers contain a chemical compound called capsaicin, which is responsible for their hot and spicy taste. Capsaicin can also provide health benefits, such as reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and potentially even aiding in weight loss. Chilli is used in a variety of dishes, including soups, stews, sauces, and marinades. It can also be dried and ground into a powder for use as a seasoning. In some cultures, chilli peppers are even eaten raw as a snack or as a condiment for other foods.
